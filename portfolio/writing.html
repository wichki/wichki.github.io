Traveling Through Seoul

October 27, 2015

Seoul is the capital city of South Korea. It is a bustling metropolis home to a population of about 25.6 million people and the 4th largest urban economy in the world, after Tokyo, New York City and Los Angeles. Seoul is rising global city with an outsize cultural and economic influence, attracting over 10 million international visitors a year, and it represents the how far the country has come since the Korean War.

Traveling To the DMZ

November 24, 2015

The Korean demilitarized zone (DMZ) is a remnant of the Korean War that bisects the peninsula into North Korea and South Korea. Created after the armistice agreement was signed in 1953, the DMZ itself is a 2 km buffer zone on both sides of the Military Demarcation Line (MDL), a line that stretches 160 miles (250 km) across the country along the 38th parallel. Despite its name, the DMZ is the most heavily militarized border in the world.
